Faces of the Crisis: The Depayin School Bombing

Behind the statistics are human stories. The May 2025 attack on a school in Depayin, Sagaing Region, is a horrifying example of the military's disregard for children's lives. This was not an isolated incident; similar aerial attacks have been reported across the country, including in Mogok.

Airstrike on a Morning Assembly

On May 12, 2025, a military jet dropped two bombs on a school operated by the pro-democracy movement. It was filled with students attending supplementary classes. The attack occurred despite the military's own declared ceasefire. Witnesses described horrific scenes of carnage, with the youngest victim just seven years old. The state-run media later dismissed the reports as "fake news," a common tactic used to deny atrocities.

This event, and others like the bombings in Mogok, Mandalay Region, around the same time, highlights a deliberate strategy to terrorize civilians and dismantle alternative education systems that represent a challenge to the junta's authority.

An Urgent Call to Action

The international response has been called "woefully inadequate." Based on the findings, human rights groups and UN bodies are demanding immediate and robust international action to protect Myanmar's children and hold perpetrators accountable.

  • Immediate Cessation of Attacks: Demand the junta end all attacks on civilians and halt child recruitment.
  • Unfettered Humanitarian Access: Allow aid organizations to reach all affected populations without restriction.
  • Targeted Sanctions & Arms Embargo: Block the flow of weapons, jet fuel, and funds to the military junta.
  • Support for International Justice: Bolster efforts to investigate and prosecute war crimes and crimes against humanity.
  • Sustained Support for Education: Fund alternative and non-formal education to prevent a lost generation.
